## Approach 1: Brute Force Summation

This is the manual method you mentioned. You would:

1. Generate all multiples of 3 below 1000: $3, 6, 9, ..., 999$.
2. Generate all multiples of 5 below 1000: $5, 10, 15, ..., 995$.
3. Add the numbers from both lists together.
4. Identify all numbers that are in **both** lists (multiples of 15) and subtract them once to correct for double-counting.

While this works, it's slow and prone to error. Your idea about sets provides the formal structure to do this efficiently.
