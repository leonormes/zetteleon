The phenomenon of getting stuck on trivial matters can be understood as a chain of interacting cognitive biases. It begins when our environment creates a state of [[Contextual Myopia]], a narrow bubble of focus. For deeper insights into thought processes and language, explore [[Thought and Language]]. For strategies to overcome overthinking, see [[Overcoming Intelligent Paralysis MOC]].

Within this bubble, the [[The Focusing Illusion]] makes us magnify the importance of whatever is immediately in front of us. This overemphasis on the immediate can lead to [[Goal Displacement]], where we begin to treat the process as more important than the actual goal.

This entire state is made dangerously stable by a feedback loop: the [[Sunk Cost Fallacy Reinforces Myopia]], causing us to double down on our narrow focus to justify the effort we've already expended.

[[Humans are susceptible to contextual myopia]]
