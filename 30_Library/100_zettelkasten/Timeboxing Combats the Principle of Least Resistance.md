Without a clear structure, it is natural to gravitate toward shallow, easy tasksâ€”the path of least resistance. Timeboxing provides the necessary structure to counteract this tendency.

By scheduling specific blocks for deep, difficult work, an individual creates a firm obligation to tackle these challenging tasks. The pre-defined schedule overrides the default behavior of choosing what is easy in the moment.

This structure facilitates deliberate choice, ensuring that important, high-effort tasks are not perpetually pushed aside in favor of more trivial activities.
