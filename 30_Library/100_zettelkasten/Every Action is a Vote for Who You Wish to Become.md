**Summary:** Every action you take is metaphorically a "vote for the type of person you wish to become." Each time you perform a behavior, you're casting a ballot in the ongoing election of your identity.

**The Voting Metaphor:**

Just as elections are decided by accumulated votes rather than a single ballot, identity is determined by accumulated actions rather than single behaviors:

- Each time you write a page, you cast a vote for "I am a writer"
- Each time you practice violin, you cast a vote for "I am a musician"
- Each time you go to the gym, you cast a vote for "I am an athlete"
- Each time you meditate, you cast a vote for "I am a mindful person"

**Why This Metaphor Works:**

**1. Accumulation Matters:**
- No single vote determines an election
- No single action determines your identity
- It's the aggregate that counts

**2. Every Action Counts:**
- Even small actions are votes
- You're always voting, whether consciously or not
- Inaction is also a vote (for the status quo)

**3. You Can Change the Outcome:**
- If you don't like the current "winner," start casting different votes
- You can shift the results over time through consistent different choices

**The Power of Small Actions:**

This metaphor reveals why small habits matter so much. They're not just small actionsâ€”they're votes that accumulate over time. Write 100 words daily, and you've cast 365 votes for "writer" in a year. That's a decisive election.

**Strategic Application:**

Before acting, ask yourself: "What identity am I voting for with this action?" This question makes the long-term consequences of immediate choices visible.

**The Tally:**

Your current identity is simply the result of which votes have won so far. Want a different identity? Start casting different votes. The new results will come through accumulated ballots.
