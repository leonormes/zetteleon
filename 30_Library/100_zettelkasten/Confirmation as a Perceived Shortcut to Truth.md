When a belief is validated by an external source, it can create a powerful subjective feeling of having found a "shortcut to truth." This feeling provides immediate relief from uncertainty, but may not reflect genuine correctness.

It functions as one of the many [[Thinking Patterns, Biases, and Heuristics in Development|cognitive heuristics]] that prioritises comfort over rigour, standing in contrast to methods like [[Mathematics Established Knowledge Through Open Demonstration|mathematical proof]].
