The Fractal Model of Self-Referential Meaning posits that the mechanisms by which humans create localized, temporary "bubbles" of importance are a micro-version of the grand cosmic process of life's emergence. The model is fractal, meaning the same fundamental pattern repeats at different scales.

It works in levels:

1. **Level 0: The Physical Universe.** The baseline reality. Governed by goalless physical laws. There is no inherent "importance" or "purpose" here, a state described by [[Emergence and the Absence of Teleology]].
2. **Level 1: The Biological Bubble.** Through emergence, [[Autopoiesis The Self-Referential System|autopoiesis]] creates the system of Life. This system is self-referential; its internally generated "purpose" is its own continuation.
3. **Level 2: The Conscious Bubble.** Within life, consciousness emerges, leading to [[The Existential Condition]]. This creates a new layer of self-referential meaning, generating abstract goals like understanding, happiness, and legacy.
4. **Level 3: The Socio-Cultural Bubbles.** Within consciousness, we create countless smaller, temporary bubbles as described in [[Contextual Myopia and Goal Displacement]]. Each level operates by the same rule: a system, blind to any larger context, generates its own internal logic and sense of importance, which is all-consuming from within but arbitrary from without.
