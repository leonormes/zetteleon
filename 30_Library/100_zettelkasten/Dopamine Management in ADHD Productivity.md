## Dopamine Management in ADHD Productivity

Because the ADHD brain operates on an [[The Interest-Based Nervous System in ADHD|Interest-Based Nervous System]], actively managing dopamine is a core productivity principle. This involves structuring work and rewards to provide the consistent stimulation the brain needs to stay engaged.

### Core Strategies

1. **The Dopamine Sandwich**: This technique structures work by "sandwiching" a boring or difficult task between two enjoyable or stimulating activities.
    - **Top Slice (Pre-Task Boost)**: Start with an activity that provides a quick dopamine hit (e.g., listening to music, a brief chat, a cup of coffee).
    - **The Filling (The Task)**: Perform the non-preferred task.
    - **Bottom Slice (The Reward)**: Immediately follow the task with a pre-defined, enjoyable reward (e.g., a short break to scroll social media, a snack, a walk).

2. **Celebrate Wins as You Go**: The ADHD brain does not respond well to delayed gratification. It is crucial to acknowledge and celebrate small steps and minor progress. This creates a continuous feedback loop of positive reinforcement, which helps maintain motivation.
    - This can be as simple as verbally acknowledging the completion of a step ("Nice, I did the thing!") or taking a brief moment to feel proud of the effort.

### Why It Works for ADHD

- **Provides Immediate Rewards**: Both strategies are built around the brain's need for immediate, rather than delayed, reinforcement.
- **Creates Positive Associations**: The Dopamine Sandwich helps build a positive association with the boring task, as it is consistently paired with rewarding activities.
- **Builds Momentum**: Celebrating small wins creates a cascade of small dopamine releases that build momentum and make it easier to continue.
- **Makes Motivation Tangible**: These strategies make the reward for effort concrete and immediate, which is more compelling for the ADHD brain than abstract future benefits.
