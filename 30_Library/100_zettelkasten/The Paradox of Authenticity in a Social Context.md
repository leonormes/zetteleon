There is a central paradox in the modern concept of authenticity. It is often defined as being "unfiltered by social expectations," yet the very values and self-awareness we use to define our "authentic self" are themselves shaped by our social context.

Concepts like kindness, pettiness, or selfishness are relationalâ€”they only have meaning in our interactions with others. A person alone on an island cannot be selfish. Our desire to be a "good person" or to grow is rooted in socially learned standards.

Therefore, true authenticity is not an escape from social context but an integration of it. It involves being honest with your own feelings while also being aware of your impact on others and taking responsibility for it. The idea of a purely individual, asocial self is a myth; we are always products of our relationships and environment.
