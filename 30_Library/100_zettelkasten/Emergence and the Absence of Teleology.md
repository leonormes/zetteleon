This note explores the relationship between two fundamental concepts that shape modern scientific understanding: the rejection of predetermined purpose and the natural arising of complexity.

## Core Concepts

[[Teleology]] - The philosophical idea that things have inherent purpose or final causes

[[Non-Teleological Universe]] - The modern scientific view that the universe operates without predetermined purpose

[[Emergence]] - How complex properties arise from simple component interactions

## The Relationship

In a [[Non-Teleological Universe]], complex systems don't arise because they're "meant to" - they emerge naturally through [[Emergence]]. This represents a fundamental shift from [[Teleology|teleological thinking]] to mechanistic understanding.

Life emerges from chemistry not because it was planned, but because the conditions allowed it to "just happen" through natural processes.
