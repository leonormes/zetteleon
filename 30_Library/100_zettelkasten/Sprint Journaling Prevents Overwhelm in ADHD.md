Sprint journaling, a technique within [[MOC - Daily Writing for Clarity and ADHD-Friendly Techniques]], is an ADHD-friendly writing method that uses short, timed bursts of writing (e.g., 5-10 minutes) to prevent [[Executive Function Challenges are Central to ADHD|overwhelm]] and leverage the brain's [[Hyperfocus is a State of Intense Interest-Driven Focus in ADHD|hyperfocus windows]].

This approach directly addresses the common [[Thinking about the task initiation problem|task initiation problem]] in ADHD, which stems from [[ADHD Causes Task Initiation Deficits Due to Dopamine Hyposensitivity|dopamine hyposensitivity]] and a higher [[The Activation Cost of Tasks is Higher in ADHD|activation cost of tasks]]. The timer creates a sense of urgency and a clear endpoint, which can help with task initiation by providing a mini-reward and tapping into the [[Predisposition to Activities Offering Immediate Gratification|predisposition to immediate gratification]]. This is a practical application of [[Mastering Time Boxing with ADHD]] and provides essential [[External Structure and ADHD|external structure]], which is crucial for neurodivergent brains as [[Structure conserves cognitive energy for neurodivergent brains]].
