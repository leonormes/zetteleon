## 1. PKM, Metacognition & The Craft of Thinking

A central theme is the ongoing struggle with the *process* of knowledge management versus the desired *outcome* of better thinking. This includes reflections on the thinking process itself, the pull towards new tools, and the frequent return to simplicity ([[Back to Simpler Ways]]).

- **Core Challenges**:
  - The meta-work of PKM becomes a form of procrastination.
  - The "Illusion of Profundity": ideas feel powerful in the head but are difficult to articulate.
  - Over-collection of information vs. the harder work of synthesis and writing.
  - Defining the [[Purpose of my PKM system]]: Is it for learning, work, or personal insight?
- **Key Concepts Explored**:
  - [[Maintaining Lines of Thought Over Time]]
  - The search for an "effortless" or ADHD-friendly system.
  - The role of LLMs as a [[My Vision of a Thought Partner]].

## 2. ADHD, Psychology & Personal Growth

This is the most significant category, exploring the emotional and practical impact of living with ADHD and Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD). The notes document a journey of self-discovery, shame, and the search for self-acceptance.

- **Core Challenges**:
  - The cycle of procrastination, shame, and fear of failure ([[Procrastination Triggers]]).
  - The intense need for external validation and the fragility of self-belief when faced with criticism.
  - A complex relationship with authority, disliking both receiving and wielding it.
  - Emotional dysregulation, where enthusiasm is fickle and cannot be relied upon for motivation.
  - [[I have a lot of shame about my life]], often linked to perceived incompetence or not meeting self-imposed standards.
- **Key Concepts Explored**:
  - [[I have only recently started to come to terms emotionally with ADHD]].
  - [[Why External Validation is So Powerful]].
  - The impact of medication (Mounjaro) on physical health, self-image, and "food noise."
  - The desire for discipline and the ability to stick to plans.
