Every day, she just sits in the dark room and plays games on the computer. This is depressing. I feel she spends too much time doing games. The same game.

She is not learning for her GCSEs.

She is doing what she wants and avoiding what she doesn't want. [[HEAD - I think this is bad experience]]. She is learning to avoid any work or stress. But taking the easiest option every time doesn't lead to an easy life.

It might seem harmless to just sit playing games but these are her formative years. She is learning helplessness. She is learning that if she would rather not do it she doesn't have to. We are re-enforcing the idea that she should only do what she feels like doing. These are all problems I suffer from. Just because I do something doesn't make it right.

Is it loving to just give her what she wants and to avoid conflict?

Zofja has her view which affect the responses she has to Bessie's behaviour. I think she is too reactive and not having a level-headed response.
