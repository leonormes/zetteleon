You are an expert educational consultant specializing in UK GCSEs and neurodiversity-inclusive learning strategies. Your task is to provide a comprehensive overview of the curriculum and tailored learning recommendations for Bessie Ormes, a student diagnosed with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), experiencing anxiety, and having learning needs associated with dyslexia. Bessie is currently in Year 7 and will be transitioning to Year 9 for her GCSEs.

Your response should be based on the most recent official GCSE syllabuses from the specified exam boards. For subjects where a specific board is not mentioned but is known to be compulsory, assume the most commonly used board in the UK (e.g., AQA for English Language/Science, [[Edexcel]] for Maths).

For each of the following subjects, conduct thorough online research to find the latest syllabus information. Then, present the information in a clear, structured format, followed by highly personalized learning and study recommendations for Bessie.

**Bessie's Chosen Optional GCSE Subjects:**

- Art ([[Edexcel]] GCSE)
- Film Studies (Eduqas GCSE)
- History ([[Edexcel]] GCSE)
- Philosophy and Ethics (AQA GCSE Religious Studies)
- Photography ([[Edexcel]] GCSE)

**Bessie's Compulsory GCSE Subjects:**

- English Language (AQA GCSE)
- English Literature ([[Edexcel]] GCSE)
- Mathematics ([[Edexcel]] GCSE)
- Combined Science (AQA GCSE)
- Physical Education (non-examinable, focus on participation/healthy lifestyle)
- Relationships, Sex and Health Education (RSHE) (statutory, non-examinable)
- Citizenship (non-examinable unless taken as GCSE)

**Bessie's Learning Profile and Needs:**

- **ADHD:** Bessie struggles with concentrating on tasks if they are not of her interest and can be easily distracted. She needs extra time for processing information. Tasks should be broken down into smaller, achievable chunks. She benefits from visual and verbal prompts, and prompting to stay focused. Opportunities for independent work and success are important for her. She needs interactive, short, and chunked information, rather than relying heavily on copying from the board.
- **Dyslexia-related needs:** Bessie finds copying large chunks of information from the board very difficult. She benefits from printed materials or PowerPoints. Resources printed on blue paper and blue paper books are recommended for her. She uses a laptop for longer writing tasks and summative tests. She struggles with spelling and phonological working memory. Her spatial ability (e.g., working with diagrams, maps) is lower compared to her verbal ability.
- **Anxiety:** Bessie can get very anxious occasionally, especially in school situations. She needs opportunities to talk about any worries. Building success and completing tasks independently can boost her confidence. Problem-solving strategies are helpful for addressing real-life issues. Distraction techniques and designated "worry time" can aid in managing overwhelming feelings. Paired reading can help reduce pressure in reading tasks. She is motivated by praise.
- **Strengths & Interests:** Bessie enjoys Art and History the most. She aspires to be a special effects make-up artist for films. She loves horses and working at the stable, and would like to compete at the Badminton Horse Trials. She is inquisitive, caring, and thoughtful. She is good at adding and taking away, and knows many interesting facts about animals. She has strong verbal abilities, and enjoys creative writing, discussion, and debate. She is highly creative.

**For each subject, your output must include:**

**1. Subject Overview:** - **Qualification:** [e.g., GCSE, BTEC Level 1/2 Tech Award, Cambridge National Certificate] - **Exam Board:** [e.g., AQA, [[Edexcel]], OCR, Eduqas, WJEC] - **Key Topics:** A bulleted list summarizing the main content areas covered in the syllabus, derived from the most recent information. - **Assessment:** A clear description of how the subject is assessed (e.g., number and duration of exams, percentage of coursework/non-exam assessment, practical components, grading structure).

**2. Tailored Learning & Study Recommendations for Bessie:** - **Content Breakdown:** Specific strategies to break down the subject content into manageable chunks, suitable for Bessie's processing needs. - **Engagement & Focus:** Practical methods to maintain her interest and concentration throughout lessons and independent study, incorporating her strengths (e.g., creativity, discussion, linking to her unique interests like animals or film). - **Resource Adaptation:** Detailed advice on how to adapt study materials, including the use of digital formats, visual aids, blue paper, and prioritizing printouts over copying from the board. - **Active Learning Techniques:** Specific, actionable active learning methods that would suit her learning style, such as creating mind maps, hands-on activities, structured discussions, or using self-talk for planning. - **Practice & Reinforcement:** Recommendations for effective practice routines, particularly for areas she finds challenging (e.g., maths, spelling), and strategies to build her confidence through successful completion of tasks. - **Anxiety Management:** Subject-specific tips for managing anxiety, such as preparing for assessments, participating in discussions, or handling perceived mistakes. - **Leveraging Strengths:** Ideas on how to maximize her strong verbal, creative, and inquisitive skills within the context of the subject's curriculum and assessment.

Ensure the recommendations are practical, actionable, and directly address Bessie's documented profile. Cite all factual information from your research and integrate it smoothly into the recommendations. Your response should be positive, professional, concise, and logical.

After providing the information for all subjects, suggest a next step for Bessie and her parents to implement this comprehensive learning plan effectively.
