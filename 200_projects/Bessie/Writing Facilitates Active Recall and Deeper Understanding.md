Writing is a powerful tool for enhancing long-term memory through two key processes: active recall and elaboration.

1. **Active Recall (Retrieval Practice):** The act of trying to remember and write down key ideas without looking at the source material strengthens neural pathways. It provides immediate feedback on what is known and what needs further review, enhancing metacognition.
2. **Elaboration:** Explaining a concept in one's own words (whether verbally or in writing) forces the connection of new learning to existing knowledge. This process forms a rich, interconnected "web of learning" that is far more effective than passive re-reading.
