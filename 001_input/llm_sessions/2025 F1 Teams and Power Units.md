Here’s the full list of F1 manufacturers and each team’s power unit supplier for the 2025 season, based on the latest available information:

## 2025 F1 Teams and Power Units

- **Red Bull Racing**: Honda RBPT
- **Racing Bulls (formerly AlphaTauri)**: Honda RBPT
- **Ferrari**: Ferrari
- **Haas**: Ferrari
- **Sauber (Kick Sauber)**: Ferrari
- **Mercedes**: Mercedes
- **McLaren**: Mercedes
- **Williams**: Mercedes
- **Aston Martin**: Mercedes
- **Alpine**: Renault

There are four engine manufacturers in 2025: Ferrari, Mercedes, Honda RBPT, and Renault. Notably, 2025 is Renault’s final year as an F1 engine supplier, with Alpine set to switch to Mercedes power in 2026. Honda RBPT continues to supply Red Bull and Racing Bulls, while Ferrari and Mercedes each supply their own teams and several customer teams. For more details, see the [Sports Illustrated F1 engine suppliers list](https://www.si.com/racing/formula-1-engine-suppliers-full-list-2025-season).

### General 2025 F1 Engine Specs

All 2025 F1 engines are 1.6-liter V6 turbocharged hybrids, a formula in place since 2014. Each power unit combines an internal combustion engine (ICE) with advanced hybrid systems (MGU-K and MGU-H), producing over 1,000 horsepower while maximizing efficiency and sustainability.

#### Key Technical Specs (applies to All manufacturers)

- **Displacement:** 1.6 liters
- **Configuration:** V6, 90-degree bank angle
- **Valves:** 24
- **Max RPM (ICE):** 15,000 rpm
- **Fuel Flow Limit:** 100 kg/hour above 10,500 rpm
- **Fuel Injection:** Direct, up to 500 bar pressure
- **Turbocharger:** Single-stage, max 125,000 rpm
- **Hybrid System:** MGU-K (kinetic, recovers braking energy), MGU-H (heat, recovers exhaust energy)
- **Total Power Output:** Over 1,000 hp
- **Fuel:** 100% sustainable fuel

### Manufacturer-Specific Notes

#### **Ferrari**

Supplies: Ferrari, Haas, Sauber

Ferrari’s power unit is renowned for its high-revving nature and strong hybrid integration, with a focus on reliability and efficient energy recovery. Ferrari engines are known for their balance of power and driveability, and the company has a long history of innovation in F1 engine technology.

[More details](https://rtrsports.com/en/blog/2025-formula-1-engine-specs/)

#### **Mercedes**

Supplies: Mercedes, McLaren, Williams, Aston Martin

Mercedes engines are famous for their efficiency, reliability, and strong hybrid deployment, especially in qualifying modes. Their power units have set the benchmark for thermal efficiency and energy recovery, often leading the field in both outright power and fuel economy.

[More details](https://rtrsports.com/en/blog/2025-formula-1-engine-specs/)

#### **Honda RBPT**

Supplies: Red Bull Racing, Racing Bulls

Honda’s hybrid power units, branded as Honda RBPT, are known for their compact packaging and aggressive energy deployment. They have been key to Red Bull’s recent success, offering strong performance both in qualifying and race trim, with a focus on rapid energy harvesting and deployment.

[More details](https://rtrsports.com/en/blog/2025-formula-1-engine-specs/)

#### **Renault**

Supplies: Alpine

Renault’s power unit is recognized for its innovative turbo and hybrid systems, with a focus on reliability and consistent performance. 2025 is Renault’s final year as an F1 engine supplier, but their units remain competitive, especially in terms of energy recovery and deployment.

[More details](https://rtrsports.com/en/blog/2025-formula-1-engine-specs/)
