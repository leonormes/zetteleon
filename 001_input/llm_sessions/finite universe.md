## The Fine-Tuning Problem

Physicists have long been puzzled by the fine-tuning problem. This is the observation that many fundamental constants of nature (like the strength of gravity, the mass of an electron, the cosmological constant) are set within a very narrow range that allows for a complex universe and life to emerge. If they were even slightly different, the universe would be unrecognisable and likely sterileâ€”perhaps consisting of only hydrogen gas or collapsing back on itself moments after the Big Bang.

The idea of a multiverse of possibilities provides a potential solution to this puzzle via the anthropic principle: we find ourselves in a universe with these specific constants because these are the constants that allow for observers like us to exist in the first place.
