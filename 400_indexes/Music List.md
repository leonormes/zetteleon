## Artists & Projects in the Same Lineage

- Boards of Canada – maintains a dreamy, nostalgic psychedelic edge with ambient beats.
- Autechre – if you liked Aphex Twin’s more experimental work, these guys go deep into rhythm deconstruction and texture.
- Squarepusher – frenetic, jazzy IDM that takes the Aphex template into its own wild territory ([Last.fm similar list](https://www.last.fm/music/Aphex+Twin/+similar)).
- μ‑Ziq (Mike Paradinas) – lush, melodic IDM often layered with psychedelic overtones.
- Oneohtrix Point Never – modern experimental/ambient electronic with warped psychedelia ([Pitchfork IDM feature](https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/10011-the-50-best-idm-albums-of-all-time/)).
- Actress – deep, hazy textures, often straddling the line between techno and abstract IDM.
- Arca – glitchy, surreal productions with a psychedelic edge.

1. Floating Points - Known for blending jazz, electronica, and classical influences, Floating Points creates intricate soundscapes reminiscent of the experimental side of electronic music.
2. Bonobo - While not entirely new, Bonobo continues to evolve his sound, incorporating elements of downtempo, trip-hop, and world music, aligning with the styles of the artists you've mentioned.
3. Tycho - Tycho combines ambient melodies with downtempo beats, creating a lush sound that echoes the atmospheric qualities of Boards of Canada and Four Tet.
4. Nils Frahm - A contemporary composer and producer, Nils Frahm merges classical piano with electronic music, producing emotive and textured soundscapes.
5. RÜFÜS DU SOL - This Australian trio blends house music with live instrumentation, creating a sound that’s both energetic and atmospheric, appealing to fans of electronic music with depth.
6. Machinedrum - Known for his eclectic style, Machinedrum combines elements of footwork, bass music, and ambient, making his work a vibrant addition to the electronic music scene.
7. Skee Mask - This artist blends ambient, techno, and jungle influences, creating intricate sound designs that resonate with the experimental nature of Amon Tobin and Aphex Twin.
8. Kiasmos - A collaboration between Ólafur Arnalds and Janus Rasmussen, Kiasmos creates minimalistic techno that features lush, atmospheric elements, appealing to fans of deeper electronic sounds.
9. Caribou - While Caribou has been around for a while, his recent works continue to innovate within the realms of electronic and psychedelic music, offering rich, layered compositions.
10. Four Tet (still relevant) - Although already established, Four Tet remains a significant figure in electronic music, continuously releasing new material that pushes boundaries and incorporates various influences.

### Widely Recognized 20th-Century Composers

- **Igor Stravinsky (1882–1971)** – Often considered the greatest composer of the century (*The Rite of Spring*, *The Firebird*) [Classic FM](https://www.classicfm.com/discover-music/periods-genres/modern/20th-century-best-composers/)
- Edgard Varèse
- **Arnold Schoenberg (1874–1951)** – Pioneer of twelve-tone/serialism [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/culture/article/20141015-20th-centurys-10-best-composers)
- **Dmitri Shostakovich (1906–1975)** – Soviet master, symphonies and string quartets [Classic FM](https://www.classicfm.com/discover-music/periods-genres/modern/20th-century-best-composers/)
- **Béla Bartók (1881–1945)** – Integrated folk traditions into modernist language [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/culture/article/20141015-20th-centurys-10-best-composers)
- **Benjamin Britten (1913–1976)** – Revitalized English opera, *War Requiem* [Classic FM](https://www.classicfm.com/discover-music/periods-genres/modern/20th-century-best-composers/)
- **Olivier Messiaen (1908–1992)** – Known for mystical, rhythmically complex music (*Quartet for the End of Time*)
- **John Cage (1912–1992)** – Experimental icon (*4'33"*) [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/culture/article/20141015-20th-centurys-10-best-composers)
- **Karlheinz Stockhausen (1928–2007)** – Electronic + avant-garde pioneer
- **Philip Glass (b)** – Minimalism master (*Einstein on the Beach*) [Classic FM](https://www.classicfm.com/discover-music/periods-genres/modern/20th-century-best-composers/)
- **Steve Reich (b)** – Minimalist innovations (*Music for 18 Musicians*)

### Other Influential and Often-cited Figures

- **Edward Elgar (1857–1934)** – Early 20th-century symphonist [Classic FM](https://www.classicfm.com/discover-music/periods-genres/modern/20th-century-best-composers/)
- **Ralph Vaughan Williams (1872–1958)** – English pastoralist
- **Sergei Prokofiev (1891–1953)** – Ballets, symphonies, concertos
