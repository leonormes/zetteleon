title: Bonhoeffer's Legacy

---

Bonhoeffer's sacrifice underscores the importance of intellectual independence. He believed that when people stop thinking, they become 'puppets.' The video challenges viewers to question their beliefs and commit to critical thinking, a radical act in a world profiting from intellectual surrender.

In his theory of 'functional stupidity', Dietrich Bonhoeffer did not define stupidity as a lack of intelligence, but rather as a **moral and sociological defect**. It is a willful surrender of independent, critical thought in response to overwhelming external pressures, such as a powerful political or religious movement.

## The Essence of the Theory

Bonhoeffer, writing from a Nazi prison cell, was trying to understand how a cultured and intelligent nation like Germany could descend into such barbarity. He concluded that the problem wasn't malice on a mass scale, but something far more dangerous: stupidity.1

He argued that under the influence of a great upsurge of power, people are deprived of their "inner independence."2 They more or less consciously give up thinking for themselves and instead align their thoughts with the prevailing power. This is not an intellectual failure, but a human one; people *allow* themselves to be made stupid.

---

## Bonhoeffer's Definition of Stupidity

Based on his writings, Bonhoeffer's definition of this specific type of stupidity has several key features:

- **It is not an intellectual defect.3** Bonhoeffer noted that there are people "of remarkably agile intellect yet stupid, and others who are intellectually quite dull yet anything but stupid."4 It is entirely separate from one's IQ.
- **It is a moral failing.5** It represents a willful refusal to engage in critical thinking and self-reflection, especially when confronted with facts that contradict one's prejudgments.6
- **It is a sociological problem.7** Bonhoeffer observed that stupidity is much less common in solitary, independent individuals than it is in people who are part of a group or movement.8 The "power of the one needs the stupidity of the other."9
- **It involves a loss of self.** In conversation with a "stupid" person, Bonhoeffer felt one was dealing "not at all with a person, but with slogans, catchwords and the like, that have taken possession of him."10 The person is under a spell, a mindless tool.11

---

## Why Stupidity is More Dangerous Than Malice

Bonhoeffer considered this type of stupidity more dangerous than outright evil for a simple reason: you can fight a malicious person, but against stupidity, you are defenceless.

He wrote, "One may protest against evil; it can be exposed and... prevented by use of force." However, against stupidity, "reasons fall on deaf ears; facts that contradict one's prejudgment simply need not be believed." The stupid person, being self-satisfied and easily irritated, will simply push facts aside and go on the attack.

Because they have surrendered their independence, they are capable of any evil and, crucially, are **incapable of seeing that it is evil**. This makes them the perfect tool for any power-hungry leader to manipulate.
