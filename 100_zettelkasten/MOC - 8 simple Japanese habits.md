Here is a summary of eight simple Japanese habits that can be incorporated into daily life to increase wellbeing, mindfulness, and social harmony. Each is a practical micro-ritual that supports clarity, order, and interpersonal mindfulness.

## The Habits

- [[Cleaning the toilet daily promotes humility and mental clarity|Clean the toilet daily]] to foster humility and clarity.
- [[Organizing shoes fosters order and self-affirmation|Organize your shoes]] to create a sense of order.
- [[Good posture supports clearer thinking|Improve your posture]] to support clearer thinking.
- [[Itadakimasu expresses gratitude for a meal|Express gratitude before meals]] by saying "Itadakimasu."
- [[Returning a chair to its place shows respect for others|Put chairs back after use]] to show respect for others.
- [[Taru o shiru is the practice of contentment with what one has|Practice "Taru o shiru"]] to cultivate contentment.
- [[Okagesamade acknowledges the support of others in one's wellbeing|Use "Okagesamade"]] to recognize your connection to others.
- [[Waking up early provides an energizing start to the day|Go to bed and wake up early]] to anchor your day with fresh energy.

## Implementation

1.  Choose one small habit to start today.
2.  Incorporate it into your morning or evening routine.
3.  Reflect on the experience and gradually add more habits as each one feels natural.
