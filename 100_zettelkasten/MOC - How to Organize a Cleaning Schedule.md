This note provides a framework for organizing a cleaning schedule, based on the advice from Melissa Maker of "Clean My Space."

## Core Principles

- [[Cleaning Routines Should Be Structured by Frequency|Structure your cleaning routine around daily, weekly, and monthly tasks]].
- [[Break Down Large Cleaning Tasks into Small Actions|Break large or overwhelming tasks into small, approachable actions]] to increase consistency.

## Task Categories

- **Daily Tasks**: [[Daily Cleaning Should Focus on Clutter Bottlenecks|Daily cleaning should focus on "clutter bottlenecks"]], and be integrated into existing routines.
- **Weekly/Bi-Weekly Tasks**: [[Weekly Cleaning Tasks Address High-Traffic Areas|Weekly tasks address high-traffic areas]] like kitchens and bathrooms.
- **Monthly and Longer-Term Tasks**: [[Monthly and Longer-Term Tasks are for Deep Cleaning|Monthly and longer-term tasks are for deep cleans]] not needed as often.

## Scheduling and Execution

- **Scheduling Methods**: [[Choose a Scheduling Method That Fits Your Style|Choose a scheduling method that fits your working style]], whether digital or analog.
- **ADHD-Friendly Tips**: For making cleaning routines more sustainable with ADHD, [[ADHD-Friendly Cleaning Strategies Focus on Cues and Transitions|focus on using environmental cues and natural transitions]].
