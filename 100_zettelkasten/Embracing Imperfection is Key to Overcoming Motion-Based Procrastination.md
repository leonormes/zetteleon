To break the cycle of motion-based procrastination, one must accept that action with imperfection is superior to perfect planning with no action.

The pursuit of a flawless start or a perfect outcome is a primary driver of procrastination, as it keeps one trapped in the seemingly safe phase of motion. The reality is that progress is only made through doing, and doing is inherently messy.

Shifting the mindset from "this must be perfect" to "this must be done" is critical. The first draft can be flawed, the first attempt can be clumsy, and the first version can be incomplete.
