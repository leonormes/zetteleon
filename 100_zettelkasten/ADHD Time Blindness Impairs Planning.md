"Time blindness" is a common trait in ADHD, characterized by a difficulty in perceiving the passage of time accurately. This is not a conscious choice, but a neurological difference that impairs the brain's internal clock. It makes planning, estimating how long tasks will take, and knowing when to start an activity incredibly difficult.
