*These are the tools your brain uses to learn, solve problems, and imagine new things.*

- **Curiosity**: Being interested in the world, asking many questions, and loving to explore new topics. (VIA Strength & National Curriculum aim)
- **Love of Learning**: Enjoying mastering new skills and adding to your knowledge, not just because you have to, but because you want to. (VIA Strength & National Curriculum aim)
  - [[Learn Like a Pro]] - Evidence-based strategies for effective learning and memory consolidation
- **Creativity**: Thinking of new and original ways to do things, whether in art, in your writing, or when solving a problem. (VIA Strength & National Curriculum: Art, Design & Technology)
  - [[Learn Like a Pro]] - How task-switching can reduce cognitive fixation and enhance creative thinking
  - [[Adhd 2.0]] - The Default Mode Network's role in making creative connections between concepts
- **Judgement / Critical Thinking**: Thinking things through properly, looking at all the sides of an argument, and not jumping to conclusions. (VIA Strength)
  - [[Oxford Guide to Effective Argument and Critical Thinking]] - Breaking down questions into sub-questions to structure thinking and argument
  - [[How to Read a Book]] - The art of reading as elevating understanding through skilled mental operations
- **Perspective / Wisdom**: Being able to see the "big picture" and give good advice to friends. Understanding what is truly important. (VIA Strength)
  - [[Thinking in Systems]] - Understanding interconnections and patterns in complex systems
  - [[How to Read a Book]] - Distinguishing between gaining information and increasing understanding
- **Literacy**: The power of reading, writing, and expressing your ideas clearly so others can understand you. (National Curriculum Core)
  - [[Writing to Learn]] - Writing as a physical tool for wrestling with facts and ideas: "It forces us to keep asking, 'Am I saying what I want to say?'"
  - [[First You Write a Sentence.]] - The craft of sentence construction and the power of precise language to "edit ourselves into something like intelligence"
  - [[How to Read a Book]] - The process of elevating understanding through skilled reading operations
- **Numeracy**: Understanding numbers and how they work in the world, from telling the time to saving your pocket money. (National Curriculum Core)
- **Digital Literacy / Computing**: Knowing how to use technology safely and effectively to learn, create, and connect with others. (National Curriculum Core)
  - [[Learn Like a Pro]] - Digital vs analog learning tools and their impact on retention
