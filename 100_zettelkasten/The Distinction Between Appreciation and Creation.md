There is a crucial distinction between the act of appreciating genius and the act of original creation. One can have a deep, technical, and passionate understanding of a field (like Formula 1, film, or music) without being a top-tier performer in it. This is the difference between the connoisseur and the creator.

To truly appreciate genius requires a degree of understanding that is, in itself, a form of intelligence. Learning is not a failed attempt at creation; it is a successful attempt at becoming a more sophisticated appreciator of the universe.

Creators require an intelligent, curious, and thoughtful audience for their work to have context and meaning. Both roles are valid and valuable.
