title: Effective Systems are Built From Tiny Initial Actions
type: concept
uid: 
updated: 
---

When creating a new system, it is most effective to start with actions that are so small they are almost laughable (e.g., one push-up, writing 50 words). This minimizes the initial friction and makes the behavior easy to adopt. Once the tiny habit is established, it can be gradually expanded.
