The relationship between thought and language has profound implications for our understanding of consciousness and the philosophy of mind, challenging traditional views and opening new avenues of inquiry.

## Key Insights

- The existence of pre-linguistic thought challenges views that equate consciousness with language
- Consciousness may be more fundamental, with language serving as a tool for expressing and shaping conscious experiences
- Questions arise about whether consciousness is a prerequisite for language or if language gives rise to consciousness
- The nature of mental representation and the role of qualia remain central questions in philosophy of mind
- The gap between thought and language provides new perspectives on these enduring philosophical puzzles

## Fundamental Questions

- Is consciousness a prerequisite for language, or does language give rise to consciousness?
- What is the nature of mental representation?
- How do qualia fit into our understanding of conscious experience?
- Can consciousness exist without language?
- How does the thought-language relationship inform our understanding of the mind-body problem?
