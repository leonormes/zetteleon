### Overview of CSOC

- CSOC protects NHS infrastructure across England, which includes around 2 million devices, 2 billion firewall transactions, and 200,000 network events each second.
- Their mission is to safeguard critical NHS services and patient data from persistent cyber threats.

### Team and Roles

- Ryan Lee, Principal of Threat Operations, oversees threat hunting and intelligence.
- Anna Evans is a Senior Threat Hunter focusing on proactive detection and response.
- Pete Robinson is a Regional Cyber Specialist, acting as a liaison between local NHS organizations and the central CSOC.

### Threat-Led Approach

- CSOC adopts a threat-led, intelligence-driven approach to focus resources on the most relevant risks.
- The objective is to protect availability and confidentiality, enabling frontline healthcare workers to deliver care securely and without disruption.
- Proactive threat hunting is emphasized to detect and stop attacks before they materialize.

### Unique Challenges of NHS Cybersecurity

- NHS operates at an immense scale with unique risks such as connected medical devices and diverse local IT systems.
- Local insight is critical for tailoring security measures, as clinical environments have specific constraints (e.g., multi-factor authentication may not be feasible everywhere).
- The regional leads provide important context and facilitate communication between local organizations and the national team.

### Services Provided

- CSOC offers extensive services, including technical monitoring, threat intelligence, incident response, and training.
- Training and awareness are available nationally at no cost, including immersive labs and cyber incident exercises.
- The goal is to provide a consistent national defense while supporting local decision-making and risk management.

### Strategic Vision and Future

- The future focus is on strengthening relationships with local teams and expanding the reach of threat intelligence and protective measures to cover all NHS assets.
- Emphasis on community learning to share threat detections quickly across all NHS organizations.
- Continuous improvement and collaboration with local teams help improve overall cyber resilience.
- CSOC is considered among the global leaders in healthcare threat detection.

### Impact and Importance

- The work of the CSOC is crucial to maintaining care delivery for millions of daily NHS users.
- Their efforts protect frontline staff during critical moments and ensure the availability of digital health services like NHS App.
